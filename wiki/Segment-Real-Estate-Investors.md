## Executive Summary

Real estate investors and house flippers represent a **high-value, data-hungry segment** that currently relies on backward-looking sales comps and property records to make forward-looking investment decisions. CivicScout's real-time building permit data, AI-classified severity, and map-based interface offer a **unique forward-looking signal** that no competitor provides in the same way.

Building permits are a **leading indicator** — they show where capital is flowing, which neighborhoods are being renovated, where demolition-driven redevelopment is happening, and where new construction will compete with existing inventory. This is exactly the kind of early-signal intelligence that gives investors an edge.

**The core value proposition:** *"See what's being built before it shows up in comps."*

**Key takeaways:**

- Local independent investors delivered **216.9% more** starter homes than builders in the sub-$300K segment, underscoring how active this community is.

**Why Chicago matters for CivicScout:** Chicago's open data portal provides one of the most comprehensive and accessible building permit datasets in the country. The city's diverse neighborhoods — from gentrifying corridors to stable suburban rings — create a perfect laboratory for permit-based investment intelligence.

### Current Data Tool Adoption Among RE Investors

- **88% of investors/owners** have started piloting AI tools (JLL 2025 Global RE Tech Survey), but only **9% have fully integrated** AI into workflows.
- The gap between exploration and full adoption represents a **massive opportunity** for purpose-built tools that deliver actionable insights without requiring technical sophistication.
- Most investors currently rely on a patchwork of tools: PropStream for comps, Zillow for broad market data, county assessor websites for manual research, and Excel spreadsheets for analysis.
- **Pain point:** No existing tool provides **real-time, forward-looking permit intelligence** overlaid on a map with severity classification. Investors are currently blind to construction activity until it shows up in sales data months later.

## Feature Requirements

| Feature | Description | Investor Value | Effort |

| **Permit volume trends by ZIP/neighborhood** | Time-series chart showing permit count over rolling 3/6/12 months per ZIP | "Is this neighborhood heating up or cooling down?" | Medium |

## Feasibility Scorecard

| Dimension | Score (1–10) | Rationale |
|-----------|:---:|-----------|
| **Product-Market Fit** | **8** | Building permits are a proven leading indicator for RE investment. Investors actively want this data but have no good way to access it. The "forward-looking vs. backward-looking" positioning is sharp and defensible. |
| **Engineering Effort** | **7** | Core permit data infrastructure already exists. Major work needed: time-series analytics, alert system, saved searches/watchlists, and property-level permit history. All achievable in 2–3 sprints. |
| **Willingness to Pay** | **7** | Investors already spend $99–699/mo on data tools. CivicScout at $29–49/mo is accessible. Challenge: must prove ROI (deals found, time saved) to convert free users. |
| **Market Accessibility** | **8** | BiggerPockets, REI meetups, and YouTube investor channels provide **direct, low-cost access** to the target audience. Chicago's investor community is concentrated and active. |
| **Competitive Moat** | **6** | Permit data is public, so no raw data moat. However, CivicScout's moat comes from: (1) multi-jurisdiction aggregation, (2) AI severity classification, (3) map-first UX, and (4) network effects from community features. PropStream/BatchLeads *could* add permit data but would require significant product pivot. |
| **Overall** | **7.2** | Strong opportunity with clear product-market fit and accessible go-to-market channels. Primary risk is that larger competitors (PropStream) eventually add a permit layer, but CivicScout has a significant head start in classification quality and integration depth. |

## Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Severity | Likelihood | Mitigation |
|------|----------|------------|------------|
| **PropStream adds permit data** | 🔴 High | 🟡 Medium | Move fast; build superior classification and UX. Position as "the permit expert" before they can catch up. Consider API partnership instead of competition. |
| **Limited geo coverage** | 🟡 Medium | 🔴 High (today) | Currently Chicago-only. Expand to top investor metros (Atlanta, Dallas, Phoenix) within 6 months. National coverage within 18 months. |
| **Free permit data = low switching cost** | 🟡 Medium | 🟡 Medium | Moat isn't raw data — it's aggregation + classification + UX + alerts. Make the product 10x better than checking county websites manually. |
| **Investor segment is cyclical** | 🟡 Medium | 🟡 Medium | Diversify across user segments (contractors, real estate agents, homebuyers). Investors are highest-value but shouldn't be the only segment. |
| **Low conversion from free → paid** | 🟡 Medium | 🟡 Medium | Gate high-value features (alerts, unlimited watchlists, permit history) behind the paywall. Deliver "aha moments" in the free tier with the map view. |
